What Tokq Adds to Synlynk

☁️

Cross-machine persistence

project-docs/ today lives on one laptop. Tokq makes it available on any machine, to any agent, with zero manual sync.

🛒

The standards marketplace

The "standards distribution engine" from the consolidated roadmap becomes real: developers publish conventions.md collections (CI rules, TypeScript patterns, domain knowledge) and others subscribe.

💰

Economic model

The gas tank gives Synlynk a native payment rail. Cost tracking becomes automatic — operations deduct from the gas tank. No more manual costs.md rows.

What Synlynk Gives Tokq

Tokq's biggest problem (Jan 2026) was the cold-start: it had no distribution channel, no user base, no way to onboard developers without a full cloud deployment.

Synlynk solves this: every developer who runs synlynk init is a Tokq prospect. The local OS is the distribution mechanism for the cloud layer. Install synlynk → use it locally → hit the sync ceiling → upgrade to Tokq.

Synlynk is the local client. Tokq is the cloud backend. Together they're one product.

How do you want to combine them?
A — Absorb

Synlynk grows into Tokq

Keep the Synlynk name and brand. Tokq becomes the cloud-sync feature at the top of the OS stack. "synlynk sync" is the bridge.

  • One product, clear local→cloud journey
  • Synlynk brand already has code/momentum
  • Tokq's marketplace becomes a Synlynk feature
  • Drops the crypto/gas-tank complexity for now
B — Two Products, One Stack

Synlynk (local) + Tokq (cloud)

Keep both brands. Synlynk is the local OS. Tokq is the cloud platform. They connect via a published protocol.

  • Tokq can serve non-Synlynk agents too
  • Larger TAM for Tokq as standalone infra
  • Synlynk stays laser-focused on local dev
  • Requires building and maintaining two brands
C — Rebrand Together

New unified brand, new name

Synlynk + Tokq together merit a new product identity that captures the full OS-to-marketplace vision. Neither name carries it alone.

  • Clean break, no legacy naming baggage
  • "Synlynk" too narrow, "Tokq" too abstract
  • The combined product is genuinely new
  • Requires naming/branding work before launch
